Purpose and Learning Focus

This formative assignment prepares you for the Summative Outlook assignment by helping you understand how Outlook is used in professional settings, not just how individual features work.

You will explore core Outlook tools and reflect on:

  • how professionals use email, calendars, and contacts to coordinate work,
  • how design choices (such as signatures and shared calendars) affect professional perception,
  • and how system output can later serve as evidence of completed work.

This is a low-stakes, preparatory assignment. You are expected to experiment, observe, and think critically-not produce a polished artifact. Grading emphasizes thoughtful reflection, not technical perfection.

After completing this assignment, you should be able to:

  • identify key Outlook features used in professional communication,
  • explain when and why calendar tools improve coordination,
  • recognize how email signatures and contact cards affect professional perception,
  • describe how shared calendars support team-based work,
  • and prepare to document Outlook output for later submission.

You are not expected to master every Outlook feature. Focus on understanding why and when professionals use these tools.
Copilot features may not be available in your environment; understanding the concept is sufficient.
