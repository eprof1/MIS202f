Purpose and Learning Focus

This formative assignment prepares you for the IoT assignments in MIS202 by helping you understand what IoT is and how IoT systems create business value-not by having you build an IoT system.

You will explore core IoT concepts and reflect on:

  • how IoT systems combine sensors, embedded devices, software, and networks to produce useful data,
  • how IoT data flows into analytics and business systems to support decisions and automation,
  • and why organizations must consider external systems, security, and OT/IT boundaries when designing IoT solutions.

This is a low-stakes, preparatory assignment. You are expected to learn vocabulary, recognize system components, and explain how the pieces fit together-not install software or troubleshoot hardware. Grading emphasizes conceptual understanding and thoughtful responses, not technical execution.

After completing this assignment, you should be able to:

  • identify the major components of an IoT system (device, sensors, embedded systems, network, external services, analytics),
  • explain the difference between software-defined and hardware-defined aspects of an IoT product,
  • describe how connected sensors generate data that can drive decisions, automation, and services,
  • recognize the role of the network fabric and OT/IT boundaries in IoT system design,
  • and prepare to analyze IoT use cases and evidence in later IoT assignments.

You are not expected to master every IoT technology. Focus on understanding how IoT systems work as business systems and why they matter for edge computing, automation, and data-driven organizations.

Important: Any software installation steps shown in later IoT assignments are provided for reference and context. You will be graded on understanding and interpretation of evidence, not on completing installations.

Exercise 1-Concepts

ref: MIS202 LinkedIn Learning

For this Exercise listen to the LinkedIn Learning course: 'IoT Foundations: Fundamentals ; ' and answer the following.

(10) 1. What makes something an “IoT system” instead of just a “device connected to the internet”?
Focus on components (sensors, embedded systems, data flow, analytics/services) and business purpose.


(10) 2. In your own words, describe the typical data journey in IoT: from a sensor reading to a business decision or automated action.
Think: sensor → device/embedded system → network → external systems → analytics → action.

(10) 3. Why do IoT designers need to think about the “network fabric” (how data moves) and external systems (where data is stored/used)?
Consider reliability, security, integration, and scalability.

(10) 4. What is the difference between the software-defined product (digital twin / software services) and the hardware-defined product (device, sensors, embedded systems)?
Explain how both are needed for an IoT solution to deliver value.
