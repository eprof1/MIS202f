Purpose and Learning Focus

This formative assignment prepares you for the Summative09 Geographic Information Systems assignment by helping you understand how GIS tools are used in professional and business settings to analyze location-based information and support decision-making-not just how maps are created.

You will explore core GIS concepts and reflect on:

  • how spatial data represents real-world locations, boundaries, and relationships,
  • how layers, attributes, and queries work together to reveal patterns and insights,
  • and how geographic context can influence business, logistics, planning, and analysis decisions.

This is a low-stakes, preparatory assignment. You are expected to explore, observe, and reason about spatial information-not to master advanced GIS software or produce professional maps. Grading emphasizes conceptual understanding and reflection, not technical precision.

After completing this assignment, you should be able to:

  • explain the essential elements of a Geographic Information System,
  • identify common examples of spatial data used in business and everyday technology,
  • describe how layers and attribute tables represent real-world information,
  • recognize how queries and spatial analysis support insight and decision-making,
  • and prepare to apply GIS concepts when working with location-based tools and data in the companion Summative assignment.

You are not expected to become a GIS expert. Focus on understanding what spatial data represents, why it matters, and how professionals use it.
The goal is spatial awareness and analytical thinking-not software mastery.

Exercise 1-Concepts

ref: MIS202 LinkedIn Learning

For this Exercise listen to the LinkedIn Learning course: 'Foundations of Geographic Information Systems; ' and answer the following.

(10) 1. Why do organizations use Geographic Information Systems (GIS) instead of simple maps or spreadsheets when working with location-based data?
Focus on analysis, relationships, and decision-making rather than visualization alone.


(10) 2. How do layers and attribute tables work together to represent real-world locations, features, and relationships in a GIS?
Think from the perspective of a business or organization analyzing geographic patterns.

(10) 3. How can poorly structured spatial data (missing attributes, unclear layers, or incorrect reference points) reduce confidence in GIS-based conclusions?

(10) 4. If an instructor, manager, or analyst could only see the final GIS output, what visible features would signal that spatial data was analyzed thoughtfully and accurately?
Do not list steps-describe observable outcomes.

Preparing for Summative09

(10) 5. How does understanding GIS concepts such as layers, spatial relationships, and queries prepare you to interpret and explain location-based evidence in the Summative09 assignment?
Focus on reasoning and interpretation, not technical execution.
