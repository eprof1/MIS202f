Purpose and Learning Focus

This formative assignment prepares you for the Summative08 Cloud Applications and Virtualization assignment by helping you understand how cloud-based tools like Google Drive are used in professional settings to store, share, and collaborate on work across devices and teams.

You will explore core Google Drive concepts and reflect on:

  • how cloud storage differs from local file storage in terms of access, sharing, and version control,
  • how organizations use Google Drive to collaborate on documents, spreadsheets, and presentations,
  • and how visible cloud artifacts can later serve as evidence of completed work and collaboration.

This is a low-stakes, preparatory assignment. You are expected to experiment, observe, and think critically-not produce polished or finalized documents. Grading emphasizes conceptual understanding and reflection, not technical perfection.

After completing this assignment, you should be able to:

  • identify how Google Drive fits into modern cloud-based productivity workflows,
  • explain how cloud documents support collaboration and access across devices,
  • recognize advantages and limitations of cloud applications compared to desktop software,
  • describe how sharing and publishing settings affect who can view or edit content,
  • and prepare to document cloud-based work for submission in the companion Summative assignment.

You are not expected to master every Google Drive feature. Focus on understanding why and when organizations choose cloud-based tools to support collaboration and productivity.

Exercise 1-Concepts

ref: MIS202 LinkedIn Learning

For this Exercise listen to the LinkedIn Learning course: 'Google Drive Essential Training; ' and answer the following.

(10) 1. Why do organizations increasingly rely on cloud-based tools like Google Drive instead of storing files only on individual computers?
Focus on access, collaboration, version control, and organizational needs.


(10) 2. How does the ability to share, comment on, and edit files in Google Drive change how teams collaborate compared to emailing files back and forth?
Think from the perspective of a group working on a shared project.

(10) 3. How can poor file organization, unclear sharing permissions, or multiple file versions reduce productivity or cause confusion in a professional setting?

(10) 4. If an instructor, manager, or auditor could only see a shared Google Drive folder or document, what visible features would signal that the files are well-organized and professionally managed?
Do not list steps-describe observable outcomes.

Preparing for Summative08

(10) 5. How does understanding cloud storage, sharing, and collaboration concepts prepare you to document and submit meaningful evidence in the Summative08 assignment?
Focus on readiness and reasoning, not technical mastery.
