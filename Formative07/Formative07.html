Purpose and Learning Focus

This formative assignment prepares you for the Summative07 Database Management Systems assignment by helping you understand why and when organizations use database software such as Microsoft Access, rather than relying solely on spreadsheets or flat files.

You will explore core database concepts and reflect on:

  • how relational databases store data without duplication or inconsistency,
  • how tables, relationships, queries, forms, and reports work together to support business operations,
  • and how visible database output can later serve as evidence of accurate data storage and retrieval.

This is a low-stakes, preparatory assignment. You are expected to experiment, observe, and think critically-not master every database feature or design a production-ready system. Grading emphasizes conceptual understanding and readiness, not technical perfection.

After completing this assignment, you should be able to:

  • identify situations where a relational database is more appropriate than a flat file or spreadsheet,
  • explain how tables and relationships improve data accuracy and consistency,
  • recognize how queries and reports allow organizations to extract useful information from data,
  • describe the role of forms in entering and maintaining data efficiently,
  • and prepare to document database work for submission in the companion Summative assignment.

You are not expected to master every Microsoft Access feature. Focus on understanding why and how relational databases support business decision-making.
Copilot or AI-assisted features may not be available in your Access environment; understanding the database concepts is sufficient.

Exercise 1-Concepts

ref: MIS202 LinkedIn Learning

For this Exercise listen to the LinkedIn Learning course: 'Access for Office 365; ' and answer the following.

(10) 1. Why do organizations move from spreadsheets to database systems like Microsoft Access as data becomes more complex?
Focus on data accuracy, duplication, relationships, and long-term maintainability.


(10) 2. How do tables and relationships improve data organization compared to storing all information in a single worksheet or file?
Think from the perspective of a business that must maintain accurate customer or transaction data.

(10) 3. How do queries and reports allow managers or employees to extract useful information without modifying the underlying data?

(10) 4. If an instructor or employer could only see the output of a database (such as a form or report), what visible features would signal that the data is well-structured and professionally managed?
Do not list steps-describe observable outcomes.
